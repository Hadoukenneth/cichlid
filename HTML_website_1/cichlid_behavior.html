The aggressive personalities of these fishes give them character, but this trait may make them unsuitable to be kept with other fishes of nonsimilar temperament. The aggressiveness do generally vary from species to species and also from individual to individual, but fortunately there are several ways to reduce the in-fighting within your cichlid community. One way to reduce the fighting is to add many hiding places for fishes to retreat in case of nippy behavior. Another thing owners can do is to have several other fishes that are either the same temperament or are larger in size in comparison to the cichlid species. This will reduce the pressure and stress on each individual fish, as they will be targeted less. The larger the cichlid's tankmate is, the less likely the cichlid will nip at it, and also the less damage the tankmate will sustain.

The intelligence of cichlids make them fun pets. Using food as a treat, many cichlid owners are also able to teach their cichlid tricks such as jumping and rolling over. A study funded by the Austraillian Research Council discovered that archerfish can differentiate between human faces. Cichlids are also one of the rare fishes that tend to their fry. Their intelligence allows them to form a complex social hierarchies within your aquarium. Within each heirarchy, the dominant fishes will exert its dominance over fellow tankmates in the form of aggression. In large communities, the crown of being the dominant fish can change overnight! The colors on the dominant fish's scales would also change into a deeper color.
